Overview

Multi-agent systems hold the promise of distributed, parallel execution in domains ranging from manufacturing to assistive care. However, enabling multiple agents to follow natural language instructions in dynamic environments remains a challenging problem due to the lack of mechanisms for grounding language into temporally synchronized, vision-aware action plans.

To overcome these limitations, we present PRISM, a hierarchical vision-language architecture that grounds natural language instructions into synchronized multi-agent workflows by dynamically injecting synchronization constraints via predicate reasoning over multimodal observations and execution history. We further introduce RLBench-COLLAB, a ten-task benchmark spanning sequential, parallel, coupled, and behavior-aware coordination.
